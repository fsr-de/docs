# Rules of Procedure of the Digital Engineering Student Council

*This translation is for information purposes only and is not legally binding.*

These Rules of Procedure came into effect by resolution of the Interim Group of the Student Council on April 7, 2026.


## § 1 Scope and Definitions

(1) These Rules of Procedure apply to the Digital Engineering Student Council (Fachschaftsrat Digital Engineering) at the University of Potsdam.

(2) Within the lecture period, working days are considered to be the lecture days, and during the non-lecture period, the weekdays Monday to Friday that are not public holidays.

(3) Winter members refers to members of the Student Council whose term of office begins in the winter semester. Summer members refers to members of the Student Council whose term of office begins in the summer semester.


## § 2 Election of Individuals

(1) The following regulations apply to all elections for individual Student Council positions. This includes elections held at the constituent meeting, as well as any subsequent elections.

(2) Separate elections are held for each office and deputy position.

(3) Elections are held by secret ballot, unless all Student Council members vote in favor of open elections.

(4) Each Student Council member has one vote in each round, which may be cast for a specific candidate or constitute an abstention.

(5) A candidate is elected to an office if they receive an absolute majority of the votes. If no candidate receives an absolute majority, additional rounds of voting shall take place. If no candidate is elected after three rounds, the Student Council may postpone the election by a simple majority vote for a maximum of seven days. Otherwise, the election will continue with one additional round of voting until a candidate receives an absolute majority of votes or the election is postponed.


## § 3 Constitution and Offices

(1) The Student Council shall convene for its constituent meeting in accordance with the election regulations.

(2) Those who previously served as deputy chairperson and deputy treasurer shall assume the roles of chairperson and treasurer, respectively.

(3) For the constituent meeting in the winter semester, the Student Council shall elect from among the winter members, and for the constituent meeting in the summer semester, from among the summer members

1. a deputy chairperson, and
2. a deputy treasurer.

(4) Furthermore, the Student Council shall elect a representative from among its members who shall primarily be responsible for representing the council at the Assembly of Student Representatives in accordance with § 9 Statutes of the Student Body.

(5) Elections at the constituent meeting shall be held in accordance with § 2 and presided over by the new chairperson.


## § 4 Division of Responsibilities

(1) The Student Council chair is responsible for presiding over meetings in accordance with § 7. The chair consists of one winter member and one summer member, including one deputy chairperson.

(2) The treasurers manage the Student Council's finances in accordance with the Student Council's decisions. They consist of one winter and one summer member, including one deputy treasurer. They shall be designated as sole signatories. They should attend the financial workshop of the General Student Committee (AStA) of the University of Potsdam.

(3) Offices specified in § 3 may be reassigned by a constructive vote of no confidence by the Student Council, or following a resignation.

(4) All other areas of responsibility may be determined by internal resolution.


## § 5 Attendance

Any member of the Student Council who is present at the meeting location or participates via telephone or other means of communication, in particular internet or video conferencing, is considered to be present.


## § 6 Meetings

(1) During the lecture period, a meeting shall be held every week in accordance with the Statutes of the Digital Engineering Student Body. During the non-lecture period and the academic christmas break, a meeting shall be held at least once every three weeks.

(2) The chair shall invite all interested members of the Student Body to the meetings via email using a public mailing list. The invitation shall be sent the day before the meeting.

(3) Where possible, meetings shall take place in a room on the Griebnitzsee Campus accessible to the Student Body. Otherwise, the meeting will be held via an online meeting tool so that the Student Body can participate.


## § 7 Moderating the Meetings

(1) During each meeting, one of the members present must act as moderator.

(2) The moderator is responsible for appointing a minute taker, managing the agenda, and ensuring that the meeting proceeds in an orderly manner.

(3) Generally, the chair will moderate the meetings. However, they may unanimously delegate the moderation of a meeting to another member.


## § 8 Resolutions

(1) The Student Council has a quorum when at least half of its members are present.

(2) Resolutions are adopted by an open vote of all members present. A resolution is considered adopted if it receives more yes votes than no votes. In case of a tie, the resolution is not considered as adopted.

(3) Notwithstanding (2), a resolution is not considered as adopted if the number of abstentions exceeds the combined total of yes and no votes.

(4) The Student Council's resolutions are recorded in the minutes.

(5) The provision of §5 (4) of the Statutes of the Student Body regarding a quorum at a second meeting on the same matter does not apply.


## § 9 Resolutions by Circular Vote

(1) In urgent cases, the Student Council may adopt resolutions requiring a simple majority by circular vote. To this end, a Student Council member shall submit a fully drafted resolution motion.

(2) Resolutions requiring a two-thirds majority may not be adopted by a circular vote.

(3) Each member of the Student Council may cast one vote on the motion. The first vote cast counts. The opportunity to vote is time-limited.

(4) A resolution by circular vote is adopted as soon as it can no longer be rejected by a simple majority. A resolution by circular vote is rejected as soon as it can no longer be adopted by a simple majority. If the resolution by circular vote has not been adopted by the deadline's end, it is deemed rejected.

(5) The resolution by circular vote must be recorded in the minutes of the next meeting.

(6) For resolutions by circular vote concerning the budget provided by the Student Body of the University of Potsdam, the requirements of § 6a of the Statutes of the Student Body also apply.


## § 10 Minutes of the Meetings

(1) The minutes of each meeting must contain at least the following items:

1. Date, time, and location of the meeting
2. Attendance list by name
3. Note regarding quorum, including the number of members present
4. Moderator
5. Minute taker
6. Description of motions and resolutions, including the distribution of votes

(2) The meeting minutes will be made available to all Student Council members no later than the next working day after the meeting.

(3) Requests for changes must be submitted to the minute taker within three working days of the meeting and communicated to all Student Council members. If no consensus is reached regarding necessary changes, the chair will decide. After the deadline has expired, the minutes shall be deemed approved.

(4) During the lecture period, the chair shall publish approved minutes within two working days.

(5) Meeting minutes must be retained for at least three years.

(6) The deadlines specified in paragraphs 2, 3, and 4 are doubled during the non-lecture period.


## § 11 Resignations and Succession Procedures

(1) Any member may resign from the Student Council for good reasons. The resignation must be submitted in writing to the remaining members, along with a statement explaining the reasons for the resignation.

(2) Withdrawal from the Student Body results in an immediate resignation.

(3) If the Student Council shrinks to fewer than four winter or summer members, at its next meeting, the Student Council shall decide by a two-thirds majority on one of the following alternatives, provided that it still consists of at least two winter and two summer members:

1. Replacement based on the election results from the semester in which the resigning member was elected. The provisions regarding election and acceptance of election in § 10 of the Electoral Code apply analogously in this regard, and the Student Council assumes the duties of the Election Committee. The term of office for replacement members ends when the term of office for the respective resigning member would have ended.
2. The Student Council may continue working with a reduced number of members if they feel capable of handling the increased workload.

If a two-thirds majority is not achieved for any of the alternatives, then alternative 1 shall take effect.

(4) If the Student Council still does not consist of at least two winter and two summer members following a replacement, or if its membership falls below this number or it is dissolved, a General Meeting must be convened to decide how to proceed. Until the General Meeting takes place, the previous members of the Student Council shall remain in office on an interim basis. The persons serving as chair on the day prior to the resignation are responsible for convening the meeting; alternatively, other members may do so.

(5) Resignations and acceptances must be announced to the Student Body via email to take effect.

(6) If members of the Student Council resign from an elected office, that office must be reassigned at the next meeting. When doing so, the division between winter and summer members pursuant to § 4 shall be observed.


## § 12 Changes to the Rules of Procedure

(1) The Student Council may adopt amendments to these bylaws with a two-thirds majority vote by all members.

(2) Within three days of the decision, the Student Council must notify the Student Body of any amendments by email. The amendments take effect the day after they are announced.
